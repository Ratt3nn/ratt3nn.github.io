The Bees of California

Why Bees Are Important

The Problems

Bees are some of the most crucial pollinators. They are responsible for 1/3 of the food we eat (fruits, nuts, veggies) and wild plant life that form ecosystems, providing food and shelter for other animals which, inturn, ensures biodiversity. If we lose bees, there's a chance that many plants would die with them.

Pesticides have become a commonplace tool used by approximately 90% of all farmers. Some are worse than others, but all have adverse effects on the biodiversity. Neonicotinoids only account for only about 1% of all pesticides used in agriculture but their overall toxicity makes them much harmful than most others. These pesticides are used as a coating on seeds(majorly corn and soybeans), this coating then leeches off into the soil, air, and water surrounding the crop, additionally the toxins persist within the plant throughout its growth cycle. It is estimated that nearly 80% of all currently endangered species are negatively impacted by the use of these dangerous chemicals. In species like bumble bees and contact is lethal as it attacks their central nervous system which leads to paralysis and death.
