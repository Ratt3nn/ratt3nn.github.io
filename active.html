How to Find the Right Seeds to Buy

What Bee-Friendly Seeds to Buy

Choose flowers and plants that provide nectar and pollen from spring through fall. Some of the best bee-friendly seeds include: Native wildflowers, Clover, Lavender, Sunflowers, Milkweed, Borage, Coneflowers, and Black-eyed Susans

Native plants are especially important because local bees evolved alongside them and depend on them for food and shelter. Planting a variety of flowers helps support honeybees, bumblebees, and other native pollinators throughout the year.

Avoid Pesticide-Treated Seeds

When buying seeds, look for labels such as: “Untreated Seeds”, “Pollinator Safe”, or “No Neonicotinoids”

Many seeds sold in stores are coated with insecticides called neonicotinoids. These chemicals can harm or kill bees by affecting their nervous systems, making it difficult for them to fly, find food, and return to their hives. Avoid plants or seeds that do not clearly state whether they were treated with pesticides. Buying organic or untreated seeds is one of the simplest ways to create a safer environment for bees and other pollinators.
